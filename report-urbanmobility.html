Goal

The project aimed to identify spatial and socio-demographic mobility patterns in the Amsterdam Metropolitan Area to support sustainable transportation planning. By clustering similar zones, the study sought to reveal hidden structures in commuting behavior and guide decision-making in urban development and public transport design.

Methods & Tools

The analysis combined geospatial data science. Key tools and methods included:

  • Python: Data wrangling, feature engineering, and cluster analysis.
  • GeoPandas & scikit-learn: For spatial manipulation and k-means clustering.
  • Principal Component Analysis (PCA): For dimensionality reduction and interpretability.
  • QGIS: For visualizing mobility patterns and validating cluster outputs.

Skills Acquired

This project strengthened my technical skillset in data preprocessing, geospatial analytics, and clustering algorithms. It also trained my ability to interpret quantitative findings into actionable planning insights. Through the integration of spatial and statistical reasoning, I gained experience in evidence-based mobility planning and exploratory data visualization.
