Beyond Student Housing: A Systems Approach to Understand the Student Housing Crisis in Amsterdam

Goal

This report aims to analyze the growing student housing shortage in Amsterdam using a system dynamics approach. The objective is to identify the key drivers behind housing scarcity and evaluate long-term policy interventions to improve student accommodation availability.

Methods

The project applied causal loop diagrams and stock-and-flow modeling using Vensim and Stella Architect. Key system elements such as supply-demand loops, construction lags, rental pressure, and feedback delays were modeled. Scenarios explored various policies including rent caps, accelerated construction, and subsidies. Sensitivity analyses and dynamic simulations were used to assess the effects of leverage points in the housing system.

Skills Demonstrated

  • System Thinking and Causal Loop Diagramming
  • Stock-and-Flow Modeling in Stella/Vensim
  • Scenario Building & Policy Simulation
  • Communication of Complex Models through Visual Narratives
