Decision Support for Flood Risk Management in Gelderland’s IJssel River Basin: Employing an Exploratory Modeling Approach within a Broader Political Context

Goal

This report evaluates the effectiveness of different decision-making strategies under uncertainty, with a focus on flood risk management from the perspective of Gelderland. The aim was to use quantitative models to assess how key assumptions—shape long-term outcomes, and to compare adaptive versus static strategies.

Methods

The report uses model-based decision analysis like exploratory modelling analysis, sensitivity analysis, and robustness evaluation. Several policy scenarios were simulated using the ema workbench and a quantitative flood simulation model. Strategies were then compared using performance indicators under different futures. Emphasis was placed on translating uncertainty into structured decision pathways.

Skills & Competencies Developed

  • Applied model-based reasoning to structure complex investment decisions.
  • Quantified uncertainty using robustness analysis across diverse policy futures.
  • Translated technical data into actionable recommendations using decision trees and payoff matrices.
  • Enhanced fluency in decision-support modelling and communicating trade-offs under uncertainty.
