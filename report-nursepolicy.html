Tackling the Problem of Highland’s Nurse Shortage - Multi Actor Policy Analysis

Goal

This project addresses the persistent shortage of nursing staff in a fictional country called Highland. The goal was to assess the effectiveness, acceptability, and implementability of policy interventions using a structured multi-actor systems perspective.

Methods

The analysis applied the Policy Analysis in Multi-Actor Systems (MAS) framework. It included:

  • Stakeholder mapping and salience assessment
  • Formulation of causal mechanisms and intervention pathways
  • Actor-impact matrices and scoring of interventions against multiple criteria
  • Evaluation of trade-offs and robustness across stakeholders

Skills & Tools

Through this policy simulation, I strengthened my capacity to:

  • Structure multi-stakeholder policy challenges and clarify institutional roles
  • Balance conflicting objectives under political and social constraints
  • Quantify stakeholder positions, intensities, and expected impact
  • Translate qualitative stakeholder knowledge into strategic decision guidance
