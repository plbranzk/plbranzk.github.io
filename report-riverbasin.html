Deep Uncertainty in Multi-Objective Optimization Leveraging Robustness Analysis to Improve Adaptive Policy Design for River Basin Management

Goal

This thesis aims to support long-term planning under uncertainty in the transboundary Zambezi River Basin by developing a framework that enables adaptive decision-making. Instead of prescribing fixed strategies, it focuses on identifying signposts and adaptive tipping points that help decision-makers adjust water management actions over time as new information emerges.

Methods

The research builds on principles from robust decision-making and adaptive planning. A structured robustness analysis was conducted using a combination of exploratory scenario building, outcome mapping, and stress-testing of strategies across deeply uncertain futures. The study presents an operational method to identify when predefined strategies may no longer be sufficient — quantified through adaptive tipping points and monitored using signposts that act as early-warning indicators.

Key methodological components:

  • Exploratory Scenarios: Plausible socio-political and hydrological futures were co-developed to account for deep uncertainty.
  • Robustness Framing: The performance of water strategies was assessed across scenarios, identifying vulnerabilities and conditional success domains.
  • Quantitative Signpost Calibration: Indicators were identified and numerically calibrated to trigger adaptive responses at appropriate thresholds.

Case Context

The Zambezi River flows through eight African countries with divergent development agendas. Large-scale investments in hydropower, irrigation, and basin infrastructure face uncertain futures due to climate variability and political shifts. This thesis contributes a planning-support tool that enhances the flexibility of international coordination efforts under the Southern African Development Community (SADC) framework.

Skills Demonstrated

  • Robustness Analysis & Adaptive Planning: Direct Policy Search, Multi-objective Evolutionary Algorithms for designing strategies that remain effective under a wide range of uncertain futures.
  • Exploratory Scenario Development: Scenario Discovery, Stochastic Streamflow Generation, Constructing and applying divergent futures to test strategy performance and failure modes.
  • Quantitative Calibration of Signposts: Identifying, monitoring, and numerically defining early-warning indicators for adaptive action.
  • Computational Modeling & Simulation: Building and running policy-relevant models to explore system behavior under uncertainty.
  • Outcome Mapping & Stress Testing: Evaluating the conditional success of strategies using multidimensional performance criteria.
  • Data-to-Insight Translation: Turning quantitative model outputs into structured policy recommendations with governance relevance.
  • Interdisciplinary Framing: Integrating hydrological, political, and institutional dimensions into a cohesive decision-support framework.
